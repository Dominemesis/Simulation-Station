Fed Up With For Honor
Friday, November 23, 2018
Ever since its launch in 2016 For Honor has been controversial with its player base. 

First there were the connection issues, not solved until Ubisoft finally set up dedicated servers. 

Then there was the turtle meta, with defense being nearly perfect, and offense being weak.  It was to the point that fights devolved into starting matches because whomever attacked first got punished for it, so the risk versus reward didn't favor offense, only defend and react.  To answer this, Ubisoft began reworking heroes, with moves that could open their opponents and deliver an offense window, and speeding up light attacks to make them less reactable. This has ushered into For Honor what is known as the "light spam meta" where all fights are nothing but spamming light attacks/bashes that guarantee a light attack follow up.  This is largely no more fun than the previous turtle meta. 

Also there are the persistant issues with balancing the warrior's better, as some fighters are just outright more capable, fun, and designed better than others.  This happens in many fighting games, but its much more pronounced in For Honor, as the disparity between the warriors that work well, and those that do not is gross.  Also, just as the game's meta has changed from a more defensive one, to a more offensive one, few of the overall cast of heroes, save those that have received reworks, have kept up, and their kits have aged poorly in almost two years the game has been out.

On top of the design and technical issues, Ubisoft faces another; For Honor's audience has lost patience and faith.  They are out of patience with Ubisoft to get these issues corrected in any manner that could be considered timely, and have lost faith that For Honor developers can even fix it. 

So For Honor may just not have much longer all things considered, as its continued development relies on the continued microtransaction spending from its audience, and they are fed up with waiting to see if Ubisoft will ever get it right.
